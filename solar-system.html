☀ Sun ☀

The Sun is the most massive object in our solar system and contains about 99.86% of all the mass in the entire solar system. It is a giant ball of hot plasma held together by its own gravity. The Sun is primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, which undergo nuclear fusion in its core, converting mass into the tremendous energy that powers the entire solar system.

Our Sun is approximately 4.6 billion years old and is roughly halfway through its life cycle. Every second, the Sun converts about 600 million tons of hydrogen into helium through nuclear fusion, releasing vast amounts of energy that travels to Earth as light, heat, and radiation.

♀ Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called Earth's "sister planet" due to their similar sizes and masses. However, Venus is a harsh and inhospitable world. It has the hottest surface of any planet in our solar system, with temperatures reaching 465°C (869°F), hot enough to melt lead.

Venus has a thick atmosphere composed primarily of carbon dioxide with clouds of sulfuric acid, creating a runaway greenhouse effect. The atmospheric pressure on Venus is 92 times greater than on Earth, equivalent to being 3,000 meters deep in the ocean. Uniquely, Venus rotates backwards (retrograde rotation) and rotates so slowly that its day is longer than its year. It is the only planet that rotates from east to west when viewed from above the North Pole.
